Non-Portal Carrier Document Collection

Training Guide for ThreeFlow Managed Groups

Overview & Purpose

This guide provides comprehensive instructions for collecting policy documents from insurance carriers without online portals. Document collection is essential for maintaining accurate records, preparing renewals, and ensuring compliance. This process involves reaching out to carrier representatives via email to request policy documents for employer groups.

Before You Begin

Required Access

  • ClickUp (MbT - ALL view)
  • Email platform
  • Carrier contact database
  • Policy information sheet
  • Letter of Authorization (LOA)
  • Broker-specific templates/macros

Key Terms

  • Lives: Number of employees covered
  • Carrier: Insurance company
  • LOA: Letter of Authorization
  • Employer Group: Company with coverage
  • Collection Status: Progress indicator
Important: You'll typically only send 1-2 emails per carrier, grouping multiple employer groups together for efficiency.

Step-by-Step Process

1

Identify Available Work in ClickUp

  1. Navigate to the "MbT - ALL" ClickUp view
  2. Look for products that meet BOTH criteria:
    • Collection Status = Not Started
    • Document Collection Owner = Unassigned
  3. Choose a carrier to work on
  4. Note the number of lives for each group
ClickUp Tip: You can use filters to quickly find all unassigned work. Filter by "Collection Status: Not Started" AND "Document Collection Owner: Unassigned" to see all available tasks.
2

Assign Yourself as Owner

  1. Select all products for your chosen carrier
  2. Assign yourself as "Document Collection Owner"
  3. Keep this window open for reference
  4. Note all employer groups and products for this carrier
Pro Tip: Work on one carrier at a time to maintain organization and ensure all related groups are handled together.
3

Prepare Your Email Request

Start New Communication

  1. Open your email platform
  2. Start a new conversation
  3. Apply the appropriate macro/template based on your broker's guidelines
Pro Tip: Reference your broker's specific macro or template guidelines for group size requirements and renewal information requests.

Create Subject Line

Action Needed: Document Request for Policy Documents [Your Broker Name]

4

Select Carrier Contacts

  1. Open carrier contact database
  2. Navigate to appropriate region/section
  3. Consider these factors:
    • Group size specifications
    • Product-specific representatives
    • Limit to 1-2 recipients for best response rate
Best Practice: When multiple people are CC'd, often no one responds. Be strategic with recipient selection.
5

Finalize and Send

Pre-Send Checklist

  • Correct template/macro applied
  • Subject line includes broker name
  • All assigned groups listed
  • Policy numbers included where available
  • Only assigned products mentioned
  • Letter of Authorization attached
  • 1-2 carrier contacts selected

Send and Track

  1. Send the email
  2. Set follow-up reminder for 1 WEEK
  3. Ensure auto-follow-up features are activated (3-day trigger)
6

Update ClickUp Status

  1. Return to ClickUp "MbT - ALL" view
  2. Find all products for the carrier you just contacted
  3. Update Collection Status from "Not Started" to PENDING
  4. Add relevant notes about outreach in comments if needed

Time Expectations

Per Carrier: 5-10 minutes
6-8 Carriers: ~1 hour
Follow-ups: 2-3 minutes each

Troubleshooting Guide

No Response Received

  • After 3 days: First follow-up (may be automated)
  • After 1 week: Manual follow-up with updated subject
  • After 2 weeks: Escalate to supervisor or try alternative contact

Common Scenarios

No Policy Number Available

Proceed without it. Include as much identifying information as possible (group name, employee count, coverage types).

Multiple Carriers per Employer

Only request documents for YOUR assigned products. Coordinate with team members handling other products.

Unclear Contact Person

Start with most relevant title. If no response, try alternative contacts in follow-up.

Quality Assurance

Completion Checklist

  • All requested documents received
  • Documents match assigned groups/products
  • Policy numbers verified
  • Documents are current (not expired)
  • ClickUp status updated to "Complete"
  • Documents properly filed/uploaded
Remember: This process is critical for maintaining accurate records and ensuring smooth renewals. Your attention to detail and timely follow-up directly impacts service quality.
